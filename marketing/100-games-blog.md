# 100 Games in the Tasern Arcade: What Just Happened

## The Tasern Arcade Just Hit Triple Digits

The Tasern Arcade now contains 100 fully playable browser-based games. Not prototypes. Not proof-of-concepts. One hundred complete games with synthesized audio, persistent saves, procedural art, and full control schemes for both mobile and desktop.

77 of those games were built in a single development session. Zero failed builds. Every major gaming genre represented. That is not a typo.

## What Makes This Different

The web3 gaming space is littered with promises. Whitepapers describing games that will never ship. Tokens backed by nothing but a roadmap slide. The Tasern Arcade took a different approach: build first, announce second.

Every game in the arcade runs on a shared engine architecture (tasern-engine.js) that handles rendering, input, audio synthesis, and save states. This means each game is self-contained but benefits from a battle-tested foundation. No external asset dependencies. No CDN calls. No loading screens waiting on someone else's server.

The result is an arcade that loads instantly and plays smoothly on anything with a browser.

## Not Simple Clones

The easy criticism of "100 games" is that they must be shallow. Look at the flagships and decide for yourself:

**Reactor Jump** is a 32-level platformer across 8 themed worlds with power-ups and boss encounters at the end of each world. **Tasern Quest** is a full turn-based RPG with multiple towns, dungeon crawling, an equipment system, and party management. **Baseball Stars** combines RPG progression with baseball simulation across 14-game seasons with full team management.

**Tasern Pinball** has a real physics engine driving multiball mechanics and combo scoring. **Spore Defense** offers 6 tower types across 20 waves on 3 distinct maps. **Rhythm Baseling** is a DDR-style rhythm game with 5 songs and unlock progression.

Then there are the deeper cuts: **Elite Trader** (space trading simulation), **Pirates of Tasern** (naval exploration and combat), **Maniac Mansion** (point-and-click adventure), and **Little Samson** (4-character platformer where each character has unique abilities).

These are games people would play even without the blockchain context.
