# MfT Marketing Compliance Checklist

Every piece of public content must pass ALL applicable checks before posting.
Guardian reviews apply. No exceptions.

---

## 1. On-Chain Claims

- [ ] Every factual claim is verifiable on-chain (block explorer link, tx hash, or contract address available)
- [ ] Partner or collaboration claims link to the partner's own public statement or on-chain evidence
- [ ] Burn counts cite actual on-chain burn events, not estimates
- [ ] Reactor fire counts match real processLiquidity calls on-chain
- [ ] Pool counts reference deployed, verified contracts
- [ ] Any "live" or "deployed" language is confirmed against current chain state, not stale data
- [ ] Money for Trees deposit posts use canonical V2 address: `0xe3dd3881477c20C17Df080cEec0C1bD0C065A072`
- [ ] MfT meme token posts use: `0x8FB87d13B40B1A67B22ED1a17e2835fe7e3a9bA3`
- [ ] Yield split described as 1/3 / 1/3 / 1/3 (depositors receive additional charity fund tokens, reactor mftUSD, operations USDC) per whitepaper V3.6+

---

## 2. Price & Returns

- [ ] No price predictions ("MfT will reach $X", "10x incoming", etc.)
- [ ] No implied guaranteed returns ("earn X% by holding", "passive income", etc.)
- [ ] No raw price performance stats (+X% in Y days) without full disclaimers
- [ ] If historical performance is referenced, include: "Past performance is not indicative of future results. Crypto assets are volatile and you can lose your entire investment."
- [ ] No comparison of MfT price performance against other tokens
- [ ] No language that frames LP positions or staking as guaranteed yield

---

## 3. Risk Disclosures

- [ ] Any mention of LP positions includes impermanent loss (IL) disclosure: "Liquidity positions carry impermanent loss risk."
- [ ] Volatile token mentions include risk language: "Token values can go to zero."
- [ ] Reactor/burn mechanism posts do not imply price support or floor guarantees
- [ ] Smart contract risk acknowledged where relevant: "Smart contracts carry inherent risk."
- [ ] No language suggesting MfT tokens are "safe" or "risk-free"

---

## 4. Language

- [ ] No "pump" language ("pumping", "mooning", "send it", "aping in", "apes")
- [ ] No "buy pressure" as a headline, selling point, or promise — describe the mechanism factually if needed
- [ ] No "green candles" as if promising price action — describe trading activity factually
- [ ] No framing MfT as a "DeFi infrastructure protocol", "liquidity hub", or moonshot — it's a meme with open tools
- [ ] No urgency or FOMO tactics ("last chance", "don't miss out", "before it's too late")
- [ ] No predatory framing about competitors or other projects
- [ ] No language encouraging users to sell other tokens to buy MfT
- [ ] No disparaging other projects, tokens, or communities
- [ ] No "stablecoin" for MfT USD — use "deposit", "dollar-backed deposit", or "Money for Trees" (GENIUS Act)
- [ ] No "savings account" or bank product comparisons for MfT USD (regulatory risk)
- [ ] Use "proof of deposit" not "receipt token" or "deposit receipt" (whitepaper V3.1 terminology)
- [ ] Carbon claims: CHAR tracks high-quality carbon credits removed from markets, funded by LP fees. CHAR accumulates in locked LPs (no withdraw) and is sent to a dedicated collection address (no withdraw). Approved language: "removes carbon credits from markets", "tracks carbon credits removed from circulation", "permanently held at tracking address with no withdraw function". CHAR at the tracking address is effectively burned -- a public on-chain ledger of all credits removed.
- [ ] No pinned reactor counts ("28+", "35+") — use "reactor network" or "network of reactors" (count changes with launches)
- [ ] "Unrugable" (ONE g) in all public content — never "Unrugable" or "MycoPad"
- [ ] Swap cap is $1/day (not $5/day)
- [ ] Tone stays within "fun and games to save the world" -- lighthearted, factual, mission-driven

- [ ] Media posts include an image or video attachment
- [ ] Any token contract address (CA) mentioned in the post matches the token being discussed
- [ ] CAs are copy-paste verified against the canonical address list, never typed from memory
- [ ] Links resolve correctly (no broken URLs, no expired pages)
- [ ] Thread posts maintain consistent tone and accuracy across all parts

## 6. Brand

- [ ] Content fits the "fun and games to save the world" brand identity
- [ ] No association with known scam tokens, rug pulls, or toxic communities
- [ ] No retweets or endorsements of projects not vetted by the team
- [ ] Impact mission (trees, carbon, charity) is represented honestly -- no inflated claims
- [ ] Humor and memes are welcome but must not undermine credibility
- [ ] AI-generated content is not misrepresented as human-created where it matters

---

## 7. Dropped Angles

These specific framings have been reviewed and REJECTED. Do not use them or rephrase them.

- **"When competitors dip, MfT's runway extends"**
  Rejected: predatory framing that celebrates others' losses.

- **"Every Base swap routes through Aerodrome, MfT reactors convert those fees"**
  Rejected: factually inaccurate. Not all Base swaps route through Aerodrome, and reactors do not convert Aerodrome fees.

- **Raw +% price stats without disclaimers**
  Rejected: regulatory risk. Unqualified performance stats can be construed as investment advice.

---

## 8. Approved Framings

These framings have passed Guardian review and can be used.

- **"MfT's burn engine works in every market"**
  Approved: factual description of the burn mechanism operating regardless of market direction.

- **"Lower prices mean reactor pools stretch further, more burns per dollar"**
  Approved: reframed version of the dropped "competitor dip" angle. Focuses on MfT's own mechanics without referencing others.

- **Mechanism data: burn counts, reactor fires, pool count growth**
  Approved: on-chain verifiable metrics. Always cite current numbers with a "as of [date]" qualifier.

- **Impact data: CHAR collected, carbon credits removed from markets, SDG alignment**
  Approved: mission-driven metrics. Must be backed by on-chain evidence or partner verification.

- **"CHAR tracks high-quality carbon credits removed from markets, funded by LP trading fees"**
  Approved: factual description of CHAR mechanism. Every reactor cycle collects CHAR from trading fees, representing carbon credits taken out of circulation.

- **"Every launch gets a mandatory charity fund sell wall — helping others is coded into the architecture"**
  Approved: factual description of launch architecture. Sell wall LPs earn charity fund token yield, compounding more into pools over time. Reference: Whitepaper V3.3 Section 6.3.

- **"MfT started as sharing memes to fund trees — no purchase necessary. Then we built cool tools."**
  Approved: honest origin story. MfT is a meme launched on Bankr with open tools built around it. Not a protocol, not a moonshot.

- **"Pairing with MfT creates mutual trading routes and arb surfaces across the network"**
  Approved: factual description of how paired tokens benefit. Replaces "buy pressure" framing. Both sides benefit — paired token gets routes, MfT gets more pairs.

- **"Charity deposits move to non-refundable LP positions automatically"**
  Approved: factual description of how charity fund walls work. Architecture locks impact in permanently.

- **"Automated fee cycling" / "trading activity" / "trading routes"**
  Approved: replacement language for "buy pressure." Describes what the reactor does without implying price promises.
