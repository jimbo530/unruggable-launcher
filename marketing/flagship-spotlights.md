# Flagship Game Spotlights - Tasern Arcade

## Deep-dive descriptions for the 10 marquee titles

---

### 1. Reactor Jump

A full-scale platformer spanning 32 levels across 8 themed worlds, each culminating in a unique boss encounter. Players collect power-ups that modify movement and combat abilities, with difficulty scaling that rewards precision timing and level memorization. The world themes range from fungal caverns to reactor cores, each introducing new hazards and enemy behaviors that demand different strategies.

---

### 2. Tasern Quest

A complete turn-based RPG built entirely in the browser. Players manage a party of adventurers across multiple towns, each with shops, inns, and quest-givers. The dungeon system features procedural room layouts with fixed encounter tables, and the equipment system includes weapons, armor, and accessories that modify both stats and abilities. Combat uses a classic command menu with attack, magic, items, and flee options, with enemy AI that responds to player party composition.

---

### 3. Baseball Stars

The depth of a sports management sim crossed with RPG progression mechanics. Players draft and develop a team across a 14-game season, with individual player stats that grow through performance. Each at-bat uses timing-based mechanics for both pitching and hitting, while managerial decisions (lineup order, substitutions, stolen base attempts) determine outcomes as much as reflexes. Season mode tracks standings, individual stats, and awards.

---

### 4. Tasern Pinball

A physics-based pinball simulation with realistic ball behavior, flipper response, and table interactions. Features include multiball activation through skill shots, combo lanes that chain together for score multipliers, and a progressive unlocking system for table features. The bumper and ramp layouts create emergent gameplay where experienced players can maintain ball control for extended combos that casual players will not discover immediately.

---

### 5. Spore Defense

Tower defense with genuine strategic depth. Six tower types (direct damage, splash, slow, poison, buff, and economy) create meaningful build decisions on each of the 3 maps. Twenty waves with distinct enemy compositions force players to adapt mid-run -- what works on wave 5 will fail by wave 12. Tower placement matters because of range overlaps, and the upgrade system forces choices between upgrading existing towers or expanding coverage. Each map has a distinct optimal strategy.

---

### 6. Rhythm Baseling

A DDR-style rhythm game where arrows scroll upward and players must hit inputs in time with the beat. Five songs at launch with varying BPM and pattern complexity. The scoring system rewards accuracy (Perfect / Great / Good / Miss) and tracks combo streaks. Unlock progression gates harder songs behind performance thresholds on easier tracks, creating a natural difficulty curve. All music is synthesized in real-time using the Web Audio API -- no audio files loaded.

---

### 7. Elite Trader

A space trading simulation where players pilot between star systems buying and selling commodities based on regional supply and demand. Economic simulation runs in the background, meaning prices shift based on player activity and random events (pirate blockades, harvest booms, mining strikes). Ship upgrades expand cargo capacity, speed, and defensive capabilities. The risk/reward loop centers on whether to haul expensive goods through dangerous sectors or play it safe with smaller margins on protected routes.

---

### 8. Pirates of Tasern

Naval exploration and combat across a procedurally-arranged archipelago. Players command a ship, managing crew morale, supplies, and hull integrity while trading goods between ports, hunting bounties, and evading or engaging enemy vessels. Combat uses broadside mechanics where ship positioning and timing determine damage output. Port towns offer repairs, crew recruitment, and cargo trading. The wind system affects movement speed and combat positioning.

---

### 9. Spore Mansion

A point-and-click adventure game built for browser play. Players explore interconnected rooms, collecting items and solving environmental puzzles to progress through the story. Multiple solution paths exist for key puzzles, and item combinations create unexpected results. The verb-based interaction system (Look, Use, Take, Talk, Combine) gives players agency in how they approach each room. Touch controls adapt the point-and-click paradigm perfectly to mobile play.

---

### 10. Little Samson

A multi-character platformer where players switch between four characters, each with fundamentally different movement abilities. One character wall-climbs, another floats, a third has ranged attacks, and the fourth has heavy armor that resists knockback. Level design requires using the right character for each section, and boss encounters demand mid-fight character swaps to exploit weaknesses. The switching mechanic transforms what could be a simple platformer into a puzzle-action hybrid.

---
