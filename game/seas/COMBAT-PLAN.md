# Seize the Seas — Combat Master Plan
*(from the 3-round design loop — references FFT, Fire Emblem, XCOM, Into the Breach, Advance Wars, Battle Brothers, Sid Meier's Pirates!)*

**Vision:** a real squad tactics game on the ship deck — up to **4 crew per side** on a roomy **12×9** hex board, every crew member head-to-toe customizable across **7 gear slots** with **ability-score gear** that genuinely reshapes who they are (a +2 STR ring raises to-hit AND damage AND carry; +2 DEX raises AC AND Reflex saves) — all bolted onto the **VERBATIM** Tales-of-Tasern d20 engine without touching one formula. Most content is already authored (`gear-ext.js`, `bestiary-sea.js`, `bestiary-dungeon.js`, `area-encounters.js`) — this is mostly **wiring** + a power ceiling + a fairness/legibility layer.

## Build order (P0 → P6)

- **P0 — Balance constants** `[NEW balance.js]` — single source of truth: `GEAR_CAPS` (toHit +4, attack +6, ac +8, maxHp +base, move +3, range +3, castingMod +4, cover +4, abilityDelta −4..+6/item, netDerived +4), `BRACKET_MULT` (feather .8 → god 1.1), and a `threat()` formula. Read by the cap clamp, the sim, and the enemy budget.
- **P1 — Turn ON 7 slots** `[items.js, gear-data.js, units.js, bestiary-sea.js]` — **MUST-HAVE (equips pt1).** Zero engine risk, biggest instant win. `SLOTS = [weapon, offhand, armor, helm, boots, ring, trinket]`; un-collide shield→offhand, helmet→helm. Flat-mod gear works with no applyEquipment change.
- **P2 — Ability-score gear LIVE + caps + migration** `[NEW stat-derive.js; items.js two-pass applyEquipment; units.js baseRawAbilities]` — **MUST-HAVE (equips pt2, ships WITH caps).** +2 STR/DEX gear recomputes derived stats; `clampGearContribution()` enforces `GEAR_CAPS` last; `migrateLoadout()` moves old saves for free.
- **P3 — Lock the caps with a focus-fire SIM** `[NEW sim/focus-fire.mjs]` — K capped attackers focus one capped tank, 2000 trials → tank survives 2–3 rounds, a kitted squad wins 55–65% vs a ×1.0 enemy budget. CI-asserts each bracket so a future caps edit fails loudly.
- **P4 — Ship-scale & multi-ship maps via GRID-CONFIG SHADOW** `[NEW grid-config.js; game.js]` — **MUST-HAVE (bigger maps).** Do NOT touch tot-engine; copy the 4 grid-reading fns into a config-aware module so the grid can be **any** size. **Scale: 1 hex = 5 ft (D&D space).** A ship = **100 ft (≈20 hexes) long × 20–40 ft (≈4–8 hexes) wide.** Size ladder: 9×7 (1v1 training) → ~12×9 (early squad) → **full single deck ~20×6–8** → **multi-ship boarding** (two decks joined by a gangplank/water gap, ~20×14) → **fleet** later. Anything past ~14 wide exceeds a screen, so this **requires the camera (P7).** Parity test required.
- **P5 — Squads of 4** `[units.js buildPlayerUnit/makeSquadBattle; encounter.js; game.js]` — **MUST-HAVE (more pawns).** checkWin/endTurn/aiTurn are already N-safe. **DEX team-phase initiative.** Enemy reuses `spawnGroup()/spawnKraken()/rollEncounter()` (already engine-ready). Solo falls back to 1v1 (fully back-compat).
- **P6 — Fairness layer** `[NEW combat-helpers.js]` — single attack chokepoint with an **exact `{hitPct, flatDmg, critPct, hpAfter}` forecast HUD** (XCOM), an **Into-the-Breach intent telegraph** ghost, focus-fire kite/screen squad AI, per-weapon crit ranges, and an AI speed-up for big fights. The accessibility win (visual, low-text) for a sight-word reader.
- **P7 — Camera: zoom · pan · recenter · follow-active** `[NEW camera.js; game.js render]` — **REQUIRED for ship-scale + multi-ship maps** (a 20-long deck, or two ships, won't fit a screen). A viewport layer over the SVG board: drag/swipe to **pan**, wheel/pinch to **zoom**, a **fit/recenter** button, and **auto-pan to the active unit** at the top of its turn. Pure render/UX — it only moves the SVG `viewBox`, never combat logic. Mobile-first (pinch + drag). Founder's stated end-state: *ship-size fighting areas + multiple ships.*
- **P8 — Vision · Line-of-Sight · Fog of War** `[NEW los.js; game.js render + combat-helpers.js]` — **your crew are your eyes.** Each owned pawn reveals the hexes in its **line-of-sight**; **walls/cover BLOCK it** (this also completes the cut "ranged can't shoot through a wall" rule). You **always see all your own pawns** (even across fog / different ships), vision is **SHARED** (all pawns' sight combines into one view), and enemies + tiles outside your crew's sight are **fogged**. **Founder payoff:** putting **one crew on each ship lights up that whole ship** → a real reason to spread the crew across a fleet. Additive + data-driven (reuses the wall/blocking terrain); fog applies on the bigger squad/ship boards, small 1v1 training stays fully visible. Forward-compatible with the multi-ship maps.

> **Sequencing:** Wave 2 wires the **foundation** (multi-enemy + 7-slot/ability gear + spell merge + area groups, per `INTEGRATION.md`) on the current 9×7 board. **Wave 3** applies the richer layers this plan adds — **P0 caps, P3 sim, P4 ship-scale/multi-ship grid-config, P6 forecast HUD + telegraph, P7 zoom/pan/follow camera.** (Single-deck ~20×6 first; multi-ship + fleet after the camera proves out.)
