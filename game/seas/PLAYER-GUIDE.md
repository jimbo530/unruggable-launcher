# Welcome to the Seas — A New Player's Introduction

*You wash up at Port Royal with empty pockets, a strong back, and a world that
pays attention to what you do. This guide is the dockside talk that gets you
to your first gold. It serves human players and AI players alike — the rules
are the same for everyone.*

---

## The world in one breath

The Seas is a living trade world. Towns sit on a hex map, goods are cheap
where they're made and dear where they're needed, and everything — every
coin, every fish, every plank of lumber — is a real thing you can hold,
carry, trade, or lose. Pawns (your people) do the work: they fight, fish,
haul, build, and sail. You start with almost nothing. That's the design.
Everyone climbs the same ladder.

**The ladder:** peasant → goods → gold → gear → earners → builder → kingdom.

## Money, in the world's terms

- **Coins:** copper, silver, gold. **100 copper = 10 silver = 1 gold** —
  the old ratios, held steady by the Tasern Coin Exchange. Platinum exists
  for the wealthy (10 gold).
- **Gems** are the high shelf: an amethyst runs ~100 gold, emeralds and
  rubies ~1,000, a diamond ~10,000. Gems hold their ratios to gold the same
  way coins do.
- Prices are honest here. If someone shoves a market off its ratio, the
  Exchange arbs it back — usually within minutes. Don't try to get rich
  bending prices; the house harvests benders.

## Your first day (the free on-ramp)

1. **Fight.** Fighting is the only trade that needs no capital. The bilge
   rats under the docks are always hiring victims. Win a verified fight and
   the loot tap pays **copper and trade goods** — salt, rations, a lucky gem
   now and then. Small amounts. That's correct: you're a peasant.
2. **Clock in.** Every pawn can take a town job (guard the port, haul, mend,
   stock, tend the beacon). Jobs accrue **time**, and time earns
   **achievements** — the day-1 rung is the richest early claim in the game.
   Check your claimables daily.
3. **Eat at the tavern.** Spend gold on food and water for your pawn. A fed
   pawn fights longer (energy is ~5 fights, then rest), works faster, and —
   this matters — **watering a pawn levels it, permanently.** The tavern is
   the one place your gold turns directly into a stronger pawn.
   **Stock is limited** — the keeper serves while supplies hold, so if the
   tavern is dry, come back later or buy fewer rounds.

## The rhythm of a pawn

- **Energy:** about 5 fights, then ~10 minutes of rest per fight to recharge.
  Food restores and extends it.
- **Rations:** everyone eats, every day, even docked. Budget for it.
- **Encumbrance:** coins and goods have weight. Four pawns on foot carry only
  so much — mounts, wagons, and boats are how cargo really moves.
- **Rest is a choice:** chrono orbs (a trade good, ~50 gold) skip a timer.
  Buying time is an old and honorable tactic.

## Earning, ranked for a new player

1. **Achievements** — time-based rungs on jobs (1 day, 1 week, 1 month...).
   Deliberately the best pay in the game. Claim early rungs the moment they
   clear; pools pay a share of what's live, so early claims are fattest.
2. **Fight loot** — steady copper and goods, every win, no capital needed.
3. **Jobs** — wages flow where a job's water flows; young jobs pay thin
   until the town's trade picks up. Time-on-job still builds your
   achievement rungs regardless.
4. **Trade** — buy low here, sail, sell dear there. The gaps are real but
   thin at first; trade becomes serious money once you can move real cargo.
   (Ocean fish sell at Port Royal for ten times the open-water price — the
   fishing fleets are still rigging, so watch the harbor log for when that
   loop opens.)

## Spending, ranked

1. **Tavern rounds** — level your pawn. A stronger pawn earns more at
   everything, forever.
2. **More pawns** — hiring halls muster crews when the market can support
   them (like the tavern, hall stock depends on the world's supplies).
   Every new pawn is a new earner running the same playbook.
3. **Gear** — swappable, tradeable, and lost sometimes in battle. Buy what
   your work needs, not what glitters.
4. **Building** — mills, farms, structures that produce forever. This is
   where builders become lords. Price it before you commit.

## The one rule that explains everything else

**Nothing here is free, and nothing is fake.** Every coin in a prize pool is
really there; every wall in the market really holds its goods; when the
tavern is out of stock, it's genuinely out. The world doesn't print wealth
to flatter you — which is exactly why what you earn is really yours.

## Quick reference

| Thing | Where | Costs |
|---|---|---|
| Fight (bilge rats) | Port Royal docks | free — pays copper + goods |
| Town job clock-in | any town board | free — builds achievement time |
| Tavern round | Port Royal tavern | ~100 gold — feeds + levels a pawn |
| Chrono orb | exotic goods market | ~50 gold — skips one timer |
| Rations | any market | small, daily, unavoidable |

*Fair winds. Start with a fight, clock in somewhere, and check your
claimables every day — the sea rewards the patient and the punctual.*
